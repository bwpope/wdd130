Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this website is to share local routes for running and cycling. It will include a home page with hero banners to establish he look and energy of outdoor running and cycling, one child page for running and another for cycling.

Audience

The audience for my site is local runners and cyclist who are new to the sport or area and are not sure where they can run and bike.

Content

Home page

Run and ride the best routes in Rexburg! Avoid dogs, traffic, and enjoy the beauty of Rexburg with our top runs in Rexburg. New to running or short on time? Checkout our under 6 miles routes. Feeling beastly? Test yourself against our epic runs!

Images for the Home page

Easy Runs

Just get out there! The hardest part of running is just getting started! These routes will help you get moving and build your endurance for longer runs. Park to Park This flat course takes you from Smith Park to Porter Park. Enjoy downtown Rexburg with plenty of places to stop and rest along the way. Both parks have a running paths around them so it's easy to add on additioanl milage. River Run Get out of traffic on this dedicated path and enjoy the scenery along the Teton River! You're bound to run into other runners on this popular trail. Great for families and pets, this run can be extended to the Nature park for additioanl fun! Temple to Temple Run between the Rexburg Temple and the Teton River Temple. For and easy Run go north to the Teton River Temple, or for a chalenge run up the hill to the Rexburg Temple, -or hey why not make it an out and back!

Images for the Page 2

Epic Runs

Test yourself against this epic runs! These three runs will test your ability to climb and go the distance. Herbet Half Run to the ghost town of Herbert Idaho while enjoying the rolling hills of the dry farms. This route starts at University and Millhollow and goes 14 miles to the end of the payment, to make it a full marathon just run back! Menan Butte This iconic run may not be long but it will have your legs and butts burrning in no time! Take the north trailhead until it connects to the service road then run counter clockwise around the caltera. Bring good shoes and water! The Beast This route is 12 miles of up and down, including the iconic summers hill apaty called "The Beast" this route also includes "The Hardest Part" segment that will make you wonder if this run will ever end!
